FORK

DESCRIPTION

It is a gardening implement, with a handle and several (usually four) short.

PURPOSE

It is used for loosening, lifting and turning over soil in gardening and farming.

USEFULNESS

The tines allow the implement to be pushed more easily into the ground, it can take out stones and weeds and break up clods, it is not so easily stopped by stones, and it does not cut through weed roots or root-crops.

DIFFERENCE

Garden forks are slightly different from pitchforks, which are used for moving loose materials such as piled hay, compost, or manure. Garden forks have comparatively a fairly short, usually wooden handle, with a "D" or "T" end. Their tines are usually shorter, flatter, thicker, and more closely spaced.
