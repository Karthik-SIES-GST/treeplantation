AJUGA

Introduction

Ajuga, also known as bugleweed, ground pine, carpet bugle, or just bugle, is a genus of 40 species annual and perennial herbaceous flowering plants in the Ajugeae tribe of the mint family Lamiaceae, with most species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, but also two species in southeastern Australia

GROWING CONDITION

Ajuga ground cover spreads through runners. It is a perfect plant for outdoor planting as it is an evergreen plant and doesn’t require any special care. These plants can grow in full sunshine or in shade. There is no specific temperature for this plant but for the best growth, 10-12 ℃ would be the best.

WHAT DO THEY LOOK LIKE

Ajuga are matt forming evergreen plants that are ideal as a ground covering plant. Bugleweed has a mass of blue and purple flowering stems that sit above a foliage of leaves, these can be as variable in colour as deep green, purple, pink and cream. Their height ranges from 2-20 inches.

SOWING AND WATERING

Best time to sow is spring or autumn. Sow it thinly. Germination time: three to four weeks. Transplant to individual pots when seedlings are strong. Transplant outdoors once further established - in the summer. Space at 12 to 15 inches (30—40 cm). Water from below. Cover the container with a plastic bag until seeds germinate.

TAKING GOOD CARE OF YOUR PLANT

This plant prefers shade, but will tolerate full sunlight but needs a good drainage. It mostly soils when grown in the shaded requires a richer, humus soil when grown in full/partial sunny areas of the garden. Ideally soil should have a slightly acidic to neutral pH. Water during prolonged dry spells if growing in a sunny area. Remove un-variegated leaves. Remove offsets/runners from young plants to prevent spread; it is best to grow Ajuga in a contained area. Growing Ajuga in a sunny area also slows down its growth/spread rate. Deadhead to prevent seed set. Divide every three years to prevent rotting crowns. Propagates by dividing clumps early in the spring or in autumn; self-seeds freely.
