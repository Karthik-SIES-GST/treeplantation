CYCAS PALM

INTRODUCTION

Cycas revoluta, is a species of gymnosperm in the family Cycadaceae, native to southern Japan including the Ryukyu Islands. It is one of several species used for the production of sago, as well as an ornamental plant and in bonsai.

APPEARANCE

The palm usually grows with a single trunk, although some old specimens make grow in clumps of several trunks. the short thick trunk is topped with a large rosette of arching leaves that grow in a circular pattern. The feathery leaves, which are dark glossy green in colour are 0.6-0.9 metre long,are deeply divided into stiff leathery leaflets 10 cm long that curl out, giving the palm its species name revoluta. New leaves come out as light green spikes that form a circle around the trunk. The leaves and inquired slowly as they grow to full length, settling into a new rosette of leaves. The female flower is a globe formed by modified leaves, which letter produces tightly packed seed head, covered with small whitish leaves. The male clone is yellowish and grows 30 to 45 cm high. the seeds that form in the female flower are 3.5 CM in diameter and become brownish red when ripe.

SIZE

This very symmetrical plant supports a crown of shiny, dark green leaves on a thick shaggy trunk that is typically about 20 cm (7.9 in) in diameter, sometimes wider. The trunk is very low to subterranean in young plants, but lengthens above ground with age. It can grow into very old specimens with 6–7 m (over 20 feet) of trunk; however, the plant is very slow-growing and requires about 50–100 years to achieve this height. Trunks can branch several times, thus producing multiple heads of leaves

WATER USAGE

Requires some watering once established. generally, trees need supplemental irrigation to get established, especially if planted after the rainy season. during the first year, irrigate in the amount of 20 to 25 litres of water twice a week. during it's second year, a tree needs to be irrigated in the amount of 40 litres per week. Beginning with the third year, when the tress get establish, they need to be irritated in the amount of 50 to 60 litres once a month in order to achieve optimal flowering.

REQUIRMENTS

Grows in full Sun or partial shade, and are drought-tolerant. they prefer rich soil, but are tough enough to grow in almost any soil. good drainage is required on the plant will rot.

MAINTENANCE

Any leaves that turn yellow or brown should be removed to reduce stress on the plant and to promote new growth.the lowest set of leaves should be cut close to the trunk if the tips begin to turn brown.
